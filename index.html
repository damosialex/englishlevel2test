Writing Practice

Develop your writing skills with these guided exercises and tips. The Functional Skills English Level 2 exam requires you to write clearly and coherently for different purposes and audiences.

Writing Tips and Guidelines

General Writing Tips

  • Plan your writing: Spend a few minutes planning your structure before you start writing.
  • Word count: Aim for approximately 250-350 words per task.
  • Paragraphing: Use clear paragraphs with one main idea per paragraph.
  • Proofreading: Allow time to check your spelling, punctuation, and grammar.
  • Purpose and audience: Always consider who you're writing for and why.

Assessment Criteria

Your writing will be assessed on:

  • Communicating information clearly and coherently
  • Using appropriate detail to suit purpose and audience
  • Using effective format and structure
  • Using correct spelling, punctuation, and grammar
  • Using appropriate language and tone

Formal Letter Format

Your address (top right)

Date

Recipient's address (left side)

Greeting (Dear Sir/Madam or Dear Mr/Mrs/Ms [Surname])

Subject line (optional)

Introduction paragraph (state purpose)

Main paragraphs (provide details)

Conclusion paragraph (summarize and state action)

Closing (Yours faithfully/Yours sincerely)

Your name

Key Tips for Formal Letters

  • Use 'Yours faithfully' with 'Dear Sir/Madam'
  • Use 'Yours sincerely' when you know the recipient's name
  • Maintain a formal tone throughout
  • Avoid contractions (write 'do not' instead of 'don't')
  • Use clear, direct language
  • Include a clear purpose in your opening paragraph

Email Format

Subject line (clear and specific)

Greeting (Dear [Name] or Hello [Name])

Introduction (state purpose)

Main content (provide details)

Conclusion (summarize and state action)

Closing (Regards, Best wishes, etc.)

Your name

Key Tips for Emails

  • Write a clear, specific subject line
  • Adjust formality based on the recipient (more formal for professional emails)
  • Keep paragraphs short and focused
  • Use bullet points for lists when appropriate
  • Be concise but complete
  • Include a clear call to action if needed

Report Format

Title

Introduction (purpose of report)

Main sections with headings

Conclusion

Recommendations (if required)

Key Tips for Reports

  • Use clear, descriptive headings and subheadings
  • Present information in a logical order
  • Use formal language throughout
  • Include facts and evidence to support points
  • Use bullet points or numbered lists for clarity
  • Summarize key findings in the conclusion
  • Make specific recommendations if required

Article Format

Headline (engaging and relevant)

Introduction (hook the reader)

Main paragraphs (develop ideas)

Conclusion (summarize or call to action)

Key Tips for Articles

  • Create an engaging headline that captures attention
  • Start with an interesting hook to draw readers in
  • Use a mix of short and longer paragraphs
  • Include relevant examples or anecdotes
  • Consider your audience's interests and knowledge
  • Use subheadings to organize longer articles
  • End with a strong conclusion or call to action

Practice Exercises

Formal Letter

Write a formal letter to your local council about improving facilities in your area. Include:

  • An introduction explaining your purpose
  • At least two suggestions with reasons
  • A conclusion requesting action
Sample Answer

123 High Street
Anytown
AN1 2BC

15th June 2025

The Chief Executive
Anytown Council
Council Offices
Main Street
Anytown
AN1 1AA

Dear Sir/Madam,

Subject: Improvements to Local Facilities

I am writing to suggest some improvements to the facilities in our local area that would benefit the community.

Firstly, the children's playground in Central Park is in need of renovation. The equipment is outdated and some items are damaged, making them potentially unsafe for children. I would suggest installing new, modern play equipment and improving the safety surfacing. This would provide a better recreational space for families and encourage more outdoor activity among children.

Secondly, there is a lack of public seating in the town centre. Many elderly residents find it difficult to shop in the area as there are few places to rest. Installing more benches along the high street would make the town centre more accessible to all residents and potentially increase footfall for local businesses.

I would be grateful if the council could consider these suggestions and include them in future improvement plans for the area. I look forward to hearing your response regarding these matters.

Yours faithfully,

A. Resident

Report

Write a report on the advantages and disadvantages of working from home. Include:

  • An introduction explaining the purpose of the report
  • Sections with headings for advantages and disadvantages
  • A conclusion with recommendations
Sample Answer

REPORT ON WORKING FROM HOME

Introduction

This report examines the advantages and disadvantages of working from home, based on recent experiences and feedback from employees. The purpose is to help inform future workplace policies.

Advantages

Working from home offers several benefits to both employees and employers:

  • Reduced commuting time: Employees save time and money by not travelling to work, which can improve work-life balance.
  • Increased productivity: Many workers report fewer distractions and interruptions when working from home, leading to improved focus on tasks.
  • Cost savings: Employers can reduce office space requirements and associated costs, while employees save on travel and food expenses.

Disadvantages

However, there are also challenges associated with home working:

  • Reduced collaboration: Spontaneous interactions and team discussions are more difficult to replicate virtually.
  • Technology issues: Not all employees have adequate internet connections or appropriate workspaces at home.
  • Work-life boundaries: Some employees find it difficult to separate work and personal life when both occur in the same space.

Conclusion and Recommendations

Working from home offers significant benefits but also presents challenges. Based on these findings, I recommend:

  • Implementing a hybrid working model that combines office and home working
  • Providing technology support for home workers
  • Establishing clear guidelines about working hours and availability
  • Regular review of the policy to ensure it meets both business and employee needs

Email

Write an email to a colleague explaining changes to a project schedule. Include:

  • A clear subject line
  • An explanation of the changes and reasons
  • Details of the new timeline
  • A request for confirmation
Sample Answer

Subject: Important Changes to Marketing Project Timeline

Dear Alex,

I hope this email finds you well. I'm writing to inform you about some important changes to the marketing project schedule that we discussed last week.

Due to feedback from the client and some resource constraints, we need to adjust our timeline. The client has requested additional research before we proceed with the campaign design, and our design team is currently working at full capacity on another urgent project.

Here is the revised timeline:

  • Research phase: Extended until 30th June (additional 2 weeks)
  • Initial design concepts: Now due by 15th July
  • Client feedback meeting: Rescheduled to 20th July
  • Final deliverables: Deadline moved to 10th August

I understand this may impact your other work commitments, so please let me know if these new dates cause any problems for you. Could you please confirm that you've received this update and whether you can accommodate these changes?

If you have any questions or concerns, please don't hesitate to contact me. We can arrange a call to discuss the details further if needed.

Thank you for your understanding and flexibility.

Best regards,

Jamie Smith
Project Manager

Article

Write an article for a local newspaper about the importance of learning English. Include:

  • An engaging headline and introduction
  • At least three main points with examples
  • A conclusion that encourages readers to take action
Sample Answer

ENGLISH SKILLS: THE KEY TO UNLOCKING YOUR FUTURE

In today's interconnected world, strong English language skills have never been more important. Whether you're looking to advance your career, expand your education options, or simply navigate daily life more effectively, English proficiency can open doors that might otherwise remain closed.

English skills are vital in the workplace. Most employers now list communication skills among their top requirements for almost any position. Sarah Jones, a local recruitment specialist, explains: "Candidates with strong English skills stand out immediately. They can communicate clearly with colleagues and customers, write professional emails, and understand complex instructions." Even in technical fields, the ability to explain your work clearly can make the difference between career advancement and stagnation.

Beyond work, English skills enhance educational opportunities. Many of the world's leading universities and online courses operate primarily in English. Local resident Mohammed Ali recently completed an online degree with an international university: "Without good English skills, I couldn't have accessed this course. Now I have qualifications that are recognized worldwide."

In our increasingly digital society, English skills also improve access to information and services. From understanding government websites to comparing products online, strong reading comprehension makes everyday tasks simpler and faster. It also helps people avoid scams and misinformation, which often target those with limited language skills.

If you're looking to improve your English skills, there are many options available locally. The Community Adult Education Centre offers courses at all levels, while the library hosts free conversation groups every Tuesday evening. Online resources like the BBC's Learning English website provide flexible learning options for those with busy schedules.

Whatever your current level, taking steps to improve your English skills is an investment in your future. Why not start today?

Writing Assessment Checklist

Use this checklist to review your writing before submission:

Content and Structure

  • Have I addressed all parts of the task?
  • Is my writing organized with a clear beginning, middle, and end?
  • Have I used paragraphs effectively?
  • Is my writing the appropriate length (250-350 words)?
  • Have I used the correct format for the document type?

Language and Style

  • Is my tone appropriate for the purpose and audience?
  • Have I used a range of vocabulary?
  • Have I avoided repetition?
  • Have I used linking words to connect ideas?
  • Is my language clear and precise?

Technical Accuracy

  • Have I checked my spelling?
  • Is my punctuation correct?
  • Have I used capital letters appropriately?
  • Are my sentences grammatically correct?
  • Have I used a variety of sentence structures?
